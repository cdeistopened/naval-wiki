## When to Use

Use this when someone has made money and is confused about why they are not happier. Or when someone is chasing wealth as though it will solve everything. The person may be financially free but physically out of shape, emotionally isolated, or mentally agitated. They have won the game they were told to play and discovered it was the wrong game.

This framework names the three things money cannot buy and resets the target.

## The Framework

Naval closed his entire "How to Get Rich" tweetstorm with a deliberate anticlimax:

> "When you're finally wealthy, you'll realize it wasn't what you were seeking in the first place."

This is not false modesty. It is an observation about hedonic adaptation. Money solves money problems. It buys freedom in the material world. It removes a category of suffering. But it leaves three categories untouched.

Naval's favorite tweet of his own, by his own admission:

> "A fit body, a calm mind, a house full of love. These things cannot be bought—they must be earned."

Each of the three must be cultivated through direct effort, regardless of wealth:

**A fit body.** Jeff Bezos still has to work out. No amount of money exempts you from the biological requirement to move, strain, and recover. Fitness is non-delegable.

**A calm mind.** Your internal mental state is not controlled by external events. It is controlled by how peaceful you are inside. Rich people can be just as anxious, agitated, and tormented as anyone.

**A house full of love.** Relationships fall apart over money, but they also fall apart over internal chaos. Naval observes that a lot of divorces happen over money, but a lot of external battles also happen because your internal state is not good:

> "When you're naturally internally peaceful you're going to pick fewer fights. You're going to be more loving without expecting anything in return."

The three are interconnected. A calm mind leads to better relationships. Financial stability removes one source of relationship stress. Physical health supports mental clarity. But the money piece is the only one you can solve with money. The other two require something else entirely.

## Example

A tech executive sells his company for $40 million. Within a year he is 30 pounds overweight, going through a divorce, and taking Ambien to sleep. He has solved all his money problems and created three new ones. The framework tells him: you skipped the three things that actually matter. Start with the body (non-negotiable daily movement), work on the mind (meditation, therapy, truth-seeking), and earn back the house full of love by showing up differently.

## Output

After applying this framework, you should be able to:

1. Name which of the three earned goods you are currently neglecting
2. Stop expecting money to fix problems that only direct effort can fix
3. Prioritize the non-purchasable over the purchasable in your daily schedule
4. Recognize that wealth is a necessary but insufficient condition for the good life
