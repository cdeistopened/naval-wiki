## When to Use

Use this when someone says "I don't have the money to start" or "I don't know the right people."
These are complaints about permissioned leverage.
The framework reveals that the most powerful forms of leverage in the modern era require no one's permission.
If you can write code or create media, you can start tonight.

Also use it when someone is choosing between career paths and needs to understand which ones give them independent power vs. which ones keep them dependent on gatekeepers.

## The Framework

Naval divides leverage into two categories based on a single question: do you need someone else's permission to use it?

### Permissioned Leverage

- **Labor** -- You need people to agree to work for you. They can quit, unionize, or underperform. You are dependent on their consent.
- **Capital** -- You need someone to give you money. Investors, banks, or customers must trust you with their capital first.

### Permissionless Leverage

- **Code** -- You can write software tonight and deploy it to millions of people tomorrow. No one needs to approve your app. No one needs to fund your server.
- **Media** -- You can write a blog post, record a podcast, shoot a video, or publish a tweet. No editor needs to greenlight it. No publisher needs to accept your manuscript.

> "Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich. You can create software and media that works for you while you sleep." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-04-17-product-media.md

### Why This Matters

This distinction explains the shift in who gets wealthy.
The old rich needed permission: capital from banks, labor from workers, land grants from governments.
The new rich need only a laptop and an internet connection.

> "An army of robots is already here. It's very cheaply available. The bottleneck is just figuring out intelligent and interesting things to do to them." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-04-17-product-media.md

### The Egalitarian Implication

Permissioned leverage concentrates power in those who already have connections and capital.
Permissionless leverage is available to anyone.

> "You don't need anyone's permission to do them, and that's why they are very egalitarian." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-04-19-egalitarian-leverage.md

Naval's career recommendation follows directly: learn to code, learn to write, or ideally learn both.
These are the two skills that give you independent access to the most powerful forms of leverage.

### The Compounding Effect

Code you write today continues to serve customers for years.
A podcast episode you record today continues to attract listeners for years.
Permissionless leverage compounds in a way that labor never can, because labor requires your continuous presence.

### The Strategic Sequence

Start with permissionless leverage (code and media) to build a track record and a reputation.
Use that track record to attract permissioned leverage (capital and talent).
This is exactly how most modern tech fortunes are built.

### The Caveat

Permissionless leverage is available to everyone, which means it is also competitive.
Millions of people can write blog posts.
The differentiator is judgment -- knowing what to build, what to write about, and which audience to serve.
The leverage is permissionless. The quality of its application still depends on specific knowledge.

## Example

A 22-year-old with no connections and no capital builds an open-source developer tool and writes a technical blog about the problems it solves.
The tool gets 5,000 GitHub stars.
The blog posts get 50,000 reads.
No one gave her permission to do any of this.
A venture firm sees the traction and offers $2M in funding (permissioned leverage, now earned through permissionless leverage).
She hires three engineers (labor leverage, now earned through capital).
The entire empire started with two permissionless acts: writing code and writing words.

## Output

After reading this, a person should be able to:
- Classify each form of leverage they currently use as permissioned or permissionless
- Stop waiting for permission and start building with code or media today
- Understand why learning to code or write is the highest-leverage career investment
- Design a path from permissionless leverage to attracting permissioned leverage
