## When to Use

Use this when someone is in a fierce competitive race and losing, or when they are copying another person's business model, content style, or career path.
Mimetic desire (wanting what others want because they want it) is the invisible force that drives most competition.
This framework shows how authenticity -- building something only you can build -- is the escape hatch.

Also use it when someone asks "how do I differentiate?"
The answer is not better marketing. It is deeper authenticity.

## The Framework

Naval's formulation is one of his most cited:

> "Escape competition through authenticity." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-05-16-competition-authenticity.md

Most competition is unnecessary.
People compete because they are imitating each other, not because they are pursuing their own path.
Rene Girard's mimetic theory explains the mechanism: humans desire things because other humans desire them.
You want to start a podcast not because you love podcasting but because you see someone else succeeding with a podcast.
You end up in a Red Queen's race, running faster just to stay in place.

> "No one can compete with you on being you." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-05-16-competition-authenticity.md

When you build something that is an authentic expression of your specific knowledge, interests, and personality, competition becomes irrelevant.
Nobody else has your exact combination of experiences, skills, and obsessions.
The product that emerges from that combination is unique by definition.

### The Epinions Cautionary Tale

Naval's first company started as an authentic product review site but got caught up competing with price-comparison engines -- a game that was not true to what they were building.

> "We should have been looking at what the consumer really wanted and being authentic to ourselves, which was reviews, not price comparison. We should have gone further into esoteric items where customers had less data and wanted reviews more badly." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-05-17-stupid-games.md

The price-comparison market went to zero because Amazon won e-tail.
Every competitor in that space won a "stupid prize" because they were playing a stupid game.

### The Redefinition Strategy

> "Become the best in the world at what you do. Keep redefining what you do until this is true." — Naval Ravikant, 2019-05-13-redefining.md

The "keep redefining" part is critical.
If you cannot be the best at what you currently do, narrow the definition until you can.
A software developer competing with millions of other developers is a commodity.
A software developer who specializes in accessibility tools for agricultural cooperatives in Southeast Asia is competing with almost nobody.

### Beyond Positioning

This is not just positioning advice.
It is a fundamental claim about how markets work.
When you compete authentically, you are not fighting for market share.
You are creating a new market category where you are the default.

The practical test: if someone could replace you with another person of similar qualifications, you are not being authentic enough.
If someone would have to replace you with you specifically, you have escaped competition.

## Example

Two content creators both make YouTube videos about personal finance.
Creator A copies the format, thumbnails, and topics of the biggest channels.
He competes directly with established creators who have larger audiences and bigger budgets.
He grows slowly and burns out.
Creator B makes videos about personal finance specifically for freelance musicians, drawing on his own 10 years as a gigging musician.
His audience is smaller but intensely loyal.
Nobody else makes this content.
He escapes competition entirely because no one else has his combination of financial knowledge and music industry experience.

## Output

After reading this, a person should be able to:
- Identify where they are competing through imitation rather than authenticity
- Recognize mimetic desire in their own career choices
- Narrow their focus until they are in a market of one
- Stop trying to "beat the competition" and start building something only they can build
